<p>The <b>greater hoopoe-lark</b> (<i>Alaemon alaudipes</i>) is a <a href="/wiki/Passeriformes" class="mw-redirect" title="Passeriformes">passerine</a> <a href="/wiki/Bird" title="Bird">bird</a> which is a breeding resident of arid, desert and semi-desert regions from the <a href="/wiki/Cape_Verde_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Cape Verde Islands">Cape Verde Islands</a> across much of northern Africa, through the <a href="/wiki/Arabian_peninsula" class="mw-redirect" title="Arabian peninsula">Arabian peninsula</a>, Syria, Afghanistan, Pakistan and India. It was formerly known as the <b>bifasciated lark</b> and sometimes as the <b>large desert lark</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-Alaemon_alaudipes_-_Avibase_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaemon_alaudipes_-_Avibase-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Taxonomy_and_systematics">Taxonomy and systematics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_hoopoe-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Taxonomy and systematics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Formerly, the greater hoopoe-lark was classified as belonging to the genera <i><a href="/wiki/Upupa" class="mw-redirect" title="Upupa">Upupa</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Certhilauda" title="Certhilauda">Certhilauda</a></i> until moved to <i>Alaemon</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Alaemon_alaudipes_-_Avibase_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alaemon_alaudipes_-_Avibase-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The name Alaemon comes from the Greek alēmōn, meaning "wanderer" (from alaomai, meaning "to wander"). The genus was established by Alexander Keyserling and Johann Heinrich Blasius in 1840.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Subspecies">Subspecies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_hoopoe-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Subspecies">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Four <a href="/wiki/Subspecies" title="Subspecies">subspecies</a> are recognized:<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><b>Cape Verde greater hoopoe-lark</b> (<i>A. a. boavistae</i>) - <small><a href="/wiki/Ernst_Hartert" title="Ernst Hartert">Hartert</a>, 1917</small>: Found on the Cape Verde Islands</li>
<li><b>North African greater hoopoe-lark</b> (<i>A. a. alaudipes</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Rene_Louiche_Desfontaines" class="mw-redirect" title="Rene Louiche Desfontaines">Desfontaines</a>, 1789)</small>: Found in northern Africa from southern Morocco and Mauritania to the <a href="/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula" title="Sinai Peninsula">Sinai Peninsula</a></li>
<li><i>A. a. desertorum</i> - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Edward_Smith-Stanley,_13th_Earl_of_Derby" title="Edward Smith-Stanley, 13th Earl of Derby">Stanley</a>, 1814)</small>: Originally described as a separate species in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Alauda" title="Alauda">Alauda</a></i>. Found in north-eastern Sudan to northern Somalia, central Saudi Arabia to southern Yemen</li>
<li><b>Eastern greater hoopoe-lark</b> (<i>A. a. doriae</i>) - <small>(<a href="/wiki/Tommaso_Salvadori" title="Tommaso Salvadori">Salvadori</a>, 1868)</small>: Originally described as a separate species in the genus <i><a href="/wiki/Certhilauda" title="Certhilauda">Certhilauda</a></i>. Found in eastern Arabia to Iraq, Iran and north-western India</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Description">Description</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_hoopoe-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Description">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AlaemonHead.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/AlaemonHead.jpg/220px-AlaemonHead.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="115" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/AlaemonHead.jpg/330px-AlaemonHead.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/AlaemonHead.jpg/440px-AlaemonHead.jpg 2x" data-file-width="752" data-file-height="392" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AlaemonHead.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Head pattern of male</div></div></div>
<p>This lark is large, long-legged and slender-bodied with a distinctive down-curved bill. The face has dark markings including a line through the eye and whisker like-lines from the base of bill running under the eye. The breast is spotted and the underside is <a href="/wiki/Buffy_(color)" class="mw-redirect" title="Buffy (color)">buffy</a> white while the upper-parts are sandy grey. The female is slightly smaller with less prominent markings and the bill is slightly shorter.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The hind claw is short and straight.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The curved upper beak has the nostril opening exposed.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The tongue is bifid at the tip.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Behaviour_and_ecology">Behaviour and ecology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_hoopoe-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Behaviour and ecology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:AlaemonTarsus.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/AlaemonTarsus.jpg/220px-AlaemonTarsus.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="135" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/AlaemonTarsus.jpg/330px-AlaemonTarsus.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/AlaemonTarsus.jpg/440px-AlaemonTarsus.jpg 2x" data-file-width="690" data-file-height="424" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:AlaemonTarsus.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Tarsus</div></div></div>
<p>Birds are seen singly or in pairs as they forage by running or walking in spurts, probing and digging the ground. They have been recorded to feed on the fruiting bodies of certain fungi.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The breeding season is mainly after the first rains, in India most records are from March to July. Late records in August when the rains were delayed have been noted in India.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The courtship display of the male consists of rising with fluttering wing-strokes and then diving down with closed wings to a perch. The slow flappy start to the song flight recall a <a href="/wiki/Hoopoe" title="Hoopoe">hoopoe</a> for which this species is named. The striking wing pattern of black wing feathers with a white base and trailing edge; and a white tail with black outer feathers are displayed in flight. The male also sings with rising and falling notes consisting of trilled whistles and clicks that have been transcribed as a <i>tee-tee-tee</i> followed by a prolonged <i>tee-hoo</i> while nosediving. The typical call is a rolling <i>zreee</i> or <i>too</i>. The nest is a cup made up of small sticks and placed on a low bush or on the ground, sometimes at the base of a bush.<sup id="cite_ref-pcr_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pcr-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The nest is prominent and often placed on an isolated bush.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  Two or three eggs are laid and both sexes take turns in incubation. They feed on insects and other invertebrates, small lizards and seeds. Young birds are capable of swift running even before they can fly. The female may perform <a href="/wiki/Distraction_display" title="Distraction display">distraction displays</a> when the young or nest are threatened.<sup id="cite_ref-hbk_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hbk-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>During the heat of the day, they may shelter in the burrows of <i><a href="/wiki/Uromastyx" title="Uromastyx">Uromastyx</a></i> lizards.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> The water loss through their skin varies with temperature and they are able to live in very arid conditions.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Their breeding is dependent on the rains and in very dry years they may not breed.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution_and_populations">Distribution and populations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_hoopoe-lark&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Distribution and populations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The wide distribution consists of several populations that have been designated as subspecies. These include <i>boavistae</i> of the Cape Verde Islands, the nominate <i>alaudipes</i> of the north African Sahara region and northern Arabia. Subspecies <i>desertorum</i> has been assigned to the populations along the Red Sea coast while the eastern population of Iraq, Pakistan and north-western India are assigned to <i>doriae</i>. Forms like <i>pallida</i> and variety <i>cinerea</i> are included in <i>doriae</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
